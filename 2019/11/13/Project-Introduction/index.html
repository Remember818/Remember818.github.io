Project-Introduction

Words count 2.7k Reading time 2 mins.

My project was the sentimental analysis based on machine learning. I designed and coded a program which could identify the sentiment, positive or negative, of an input short texts. Also, I compared the effectiveness of up-to-date method, machine learning, and that of old method, SVM linear classifier.

My target was to build a highly accurate neural network that could judge sentiments. I chose LSTM neural network but not SVM, as it was more advanced and efficient comparing with SVM. In my work, I tuned various parameters of the neural network in order to achieve its best performance. In addition, I spent extra time in coding a SVM linear classifier for comparison with LSTM in order to demonstrate the superiority of LSTM.

Undoubtedly, I confronted two big challenges in this project. First and foremost, as I was a beginner in programming with python, it was hard for me to build the neural network on my own. Therefore, I must take courses online to learn the basic skills in programming, and spent time in coding for different mock situations or applications as practices. Apart from that, programming required strong ability in logical thinking, or your programs might be in chaos. Thus, it was quite hard at first in taking online course since I needed more time to completely understand the codes of the lecturer. All I could do was to strengthen my logical thinking ability, and read others’ codes online, trying to understand them.

Secondly, writing report was a laborious process. I was inexperienced in this work. Writing a long, college-level report was really challenging. I did not know the format, let alone the management of contents. So, I had to many myriad reports of university students and tried to imitate their formats. Though I finished my report, I believed that there were a lot for me to learn.

Comparing with reports in other fields like biology or chemistry, I think that my report was intriguing in that you could interact with it. You could input a random short text and the program can judge its sentiment automatically, as it was already trained with data.

The result of this project has shown that LSTM excel old SVM method. LSTM has an accuracy of 89% while SVM has only 79% accuracy. Though LSTM is not completely accurate, it is true that LSTM is more efficient and could save our time in dealing with huge amount of data.

I have learned a lot in this project. First, It laid a strong foundation for me in my future study in programming, and trained my logicality. It also ignited my interest in data-related majors, as I could see that data was becoming an crucial resources in this world.
