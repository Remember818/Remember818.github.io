Introduction

With Internet getting more and more accessible to people nowadays, they leave a tremendous amount comments online. With the traces left in these comments, it is possible to determine the attitudes of those people to some certain topics. For example, in the comment section on e-commercial website, comments are classified according to their attitudes: positive, negative or neutral, to give a quick assessment of a product. This is a laborious work. To save cost of time and workforce, deep learning is applied, and sentimental analysis based on neural network is developed.

Here are some commonly used models in classification problem. One classic method is SVM. In simple term, we trained the model with given dataset, then fit a hyperplane to separate the data into 2 or more classes. This is a supervised training process, or there will be many hyperplanes to be found. In determining which hyperplane is the one we required it considers some crucial features relating to both the line and the data dots, for example loss. Then, it could find the optimum hyperplane. Nevertheless, SVM does not perform well with large data sets, or when the data set has many noise points.

Obviously, SVM is not suitable for this big data era, and it consequently becomes outdated. People started to seek for better method of classification, and soon they put their eyes on neural network.

With faster computer and more data, human try to stimulate their brains, that is, deep learning. Somebody did a good job with deep learning on text classification problem. Deep learning’s goal is to build a complex neural network, like out brain, in the computer. Each neuron has its own preference, called weights; its own stereotype, called bias; etc. We feed it with data; it learns like a child; finally, it judges like a human.

Developing upon neural network, we now come to LSTM, which is a branch of deep learning. It is an artificial recurrent neural network. Unlike conventional neural network, LSTM has memory of the data it has processed so that it can compute with a sequence of data rather than a single data. Also, as it is developed upon the RNN, the problem of vanishing gradient is solved due to the specific cell implemented in the network: input gate, output gate, and forget gate.

Architecture

A. SVM

SVM is a simple linear classifier. It learns to classify new data by training on known data and develop a hyperplane. In order to optimize this hyperplane and simplify the process, we need to tune hour parameters: kernel, regularizer, gamma, and margin.

The diagrams below illustrate what kernel is. It is a process of computing a complex, high-dimensional problem in a simple way, like converting sentence into vector. For instance, using bag-of words method, we can express a sentence in a series of numbers.


(Text to points)

After converting complex data into simple form, we need to teach the program a hyperplane. We need to consider how conforming the hyperplane is with the data. With regularizer, we can achieve this. The left diagram shows a hyperplane with low regularization value while the right diagram shows a hyperplane with higher regularization. The regularization value determines how fitting the hyperplane is.


(Low regularization value & High regularization value)

Gamma is another value we need to control. It determines which of the data or points the model will consider and how the hyperplane will be influenced. Low gamma will make the model consider far away points whilst high gamma will make the model consider nearby points only.


(Low gamma & High gamma)

Finally, the model need to have a good margin. A good margin indicates that the hyperplane locates at the middle between two classes and will not incline to any class, while a bad margin means that the hyperplane incline to one of the classes, resulting in inaccuracy in testing or practical use.


(Good margin & Bad margin)

B. Neural Network

Deep learning, or neural network, is a branch of machine learning. Similarly, its aim is to learn a particular process and develop the most suitable model for the process. In addition, neural network tries to simulate the mechanism of human brain. Different functions are recognized as different neurons. There are several important compositions of neural network.

First, the input layer is where we feed in the data. It will then pass the data as outputs to the next layer, after computing with in-built function. The next layer receives the outputs from previous layer as its inputs and keep computing it. This process repeats over and over again, and the information is transferred in the network. Finally, it will come out as a desired output.

During the transfer of information, except the functions in each neuron, the weights will also affect the inputs. It indicates the importance of the inputs; it is the coefficients of the functions. During the training, the weights are constantly optimized. In another word, the purpose of training is to find a optimal weights for the model to make right judgement.

In each layer, there is a independent constant called bias. It is an addition to the result of the function. By its value, the results are shifted. Also, it ensures that the network can still work even when the input is zero. It is like the the y-intercept of slope-intercept form, which is constant and ensure that y has a value even if the x is equal to zero.

C. LSTM

(Conventional RNN)

LSTM is a branch of RNN. The diagram above demonstrate what a conventional RNN looks like. Comparing with feedforward network, the RNN not only produce an output, but also recurrent the output in its network, creating a kind of memory. Thus, it is widely used to deal with sequence of information such as picture, handwriting, etc. However, there is problem. RNN cannot correct the error in the output immediately and the error will also be sent back into the loop. As a result, the error will be multiplied, ultimately resulting in extreme output. This is vanishing gradients problem.

To solve this problem, LSTM is developed. Comparing with traditional RNN, LSTM is added with three more independent gates, which can control the recurrent flow like a door, called input gate, forget gate, and output gate. The diagram above illustrate how they work. The input gate decides whether to take new input or circulate the old output; the forget gate determine whether erase or keep the information in the circulation; the output gate judge whether produce an output or send it back into the loop. The gates work together, block or pass input, and maintain a constant error, in order to avoid vanishing gradient problem.

Dataset

From Figure Eight, I obtained two data sets. One was about the sentiments towards the weather of Twitter users, and the other one was about the sentiments towards various airlines. I first tokenized the texts, and then filtered all neutral sentiments of both data sets.

Overall, the “Weather” data set included less data. Excluded the neutral sentiments, about 1000 texts are within. The “Airline” data set included more data. Considering only the positive and negative sentiments, around 11000 texts are within.

I lastly divided both data set into training set and testing set at a ratio of 7 to 3.

Experimental Setup and Results

A. Model Setting
As it was impossible to compare the effectiveness of all the models in history, I planed to compare the efficiency of traditional and up-to-date methods of classification: SVM classifier and LSTM neural network. In addition, other factors affecting the accuracy of the performance, such as data size, activation, number of layers, will be tuned, in order to find the optimum setting for the performance of the classifier with different data size.

Overall, the neural network was composed of one input layer, one embedding layer, one LSTM layer, two dense layers, two activation layers and one dropout layer.

B. Regularization
For SVM classifier, I just trained and tested it with different kernel with different sizes of data sets. Whereas for LSTM neural network, I varied the dropout rate between 0 and 0.5, varied the embedding layer between 100 and 10000, and varied the LSTM layer between 16 and 64.

C. Training and Validation
Before training I implemented Tokenizer, which ignores the grammar of words, and implements bag-of-word, to convert the text into arrays. Then, I trained both the classifiers with the converted texts.

For SVM, two kernels were tested: rbf kernel which was a non-linear kernel, and linear kernel. Time and accuracy were recorded.

For LSTM neural network, I noticed that it performed better with the dropout rate 0.2 and LSTM layer value of 32. Any increase or decrease will reduce the accuracy. Considering the embedding, I found that the more the layers, the higher the accuracy. However, the time required and load on the computer would increase dramatically. Thus, I believed that 1000 embedding layers would be enough.

D. Result and Discussion
I performed the training and testing for several times in order to compare the effectiveness of traditional method and advanced method. As shown in the table above, LSTM neural network performed significantly better than SVM did.

After tuning, with the best setting for the neural network, the accuracy of LSTM reached 89% in testing. However, in the “Airline” data set, as the amount of negative comment was significantly greater than that of positive comments, the accuracy for judging positive comment was only 75% whilst the accuracy for judging negative comments was 94%.

For SVM, it was obvious that its performance was worse than that of LSTM. With rbf kernel, the accuracy in classifying “Airline” data set was 80%, while the accuracy in classifying “Weather” data set was only 50%. Similarly, with linear kernel, the accuracy for classifying both data sets were almost the same. Nevertheless, the computing times under linear kernel were much higher than other models or kernels. In training with “Airline” data set, the training time was more than 2 hours, whereas in training with “Weather” data set, a smaller data set, the training time reached 18 minutes.

Therefore, it can be seen that LSTM performed better than traditional classifier, as it computed faster with higher accuracy.

Conclusion

In this report, I examined and compared the performances of new and old classifiers, based on data set about Twitter users. I found a better way of tuning the neural network and achieved a better result. It was obvious that advanced LSTM performed better and more efficiently than SVM linear classifier.

This definitely showed the trend towards solving data problem with neural network and machine learning.
